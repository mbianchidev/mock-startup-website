Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our platform. Click on a question to see the answer.

How does pay-per-use billing work?

You only pay for the actual Matteo compute hours your workloads use. Billing is calculated per hour with minute-level precision. When your services scale to zero, you pay nothing. No upfront costs, no monthly minimums.

Can I switch between compute tiers?

Yes! You can change your compute tier at any time. Different workloads can even use different tiers simultaneously. Your billing will reflect the actual tier used for each compute hour.

What's included in the hourly rate?

The hourly rate includes all platform features for your tier, infrastructure management, monitoring, security, and support. There are no hidden fees or additional charges for data transfer within the same cloud region.

How do you handle multi-cloud deployments?

Our Production and Enterprise tiers include tooling for unified management across different cloud providers. Billing is calculated per compute hour regardless of which cloud provider hosts your workloads.

Do you offer volume discounts?

Yes! For organizations using at least 160 compute hours per month, we offer custom pricing with volume discounts. Contact our sales team to discuss enterprise pricing that scales with your usage.

How does support work with pay-per-use?

Support level depends on your compute tier. Development tier includes community support, Production tier gets business hours support, and Enterprise tier includes 24/7 priority support with dedicated account management.

Can I estimate my monthly costs?

Absolutely! We provide cost estimation tools and usage analytics. Most development workloads average 20-40 hours/week (€3,000-€6,000), while production workloads typically run 100-150 hours/month (€15,000-€22,500).

What technologies and tools does your platform integrate with?

Our platform natively integrates with modern DevOps and cloud-native technologies including Kubernetes, OpenTofu, Pulumi, Ansible, GitLab, GitHub Actions, ArgoCD, Helm, Prometheus, Grafana, and all major cloud providers (AWS, Azure, GCP). We focus on open-source solutions and industry-standard tools.

What career opportunities and growth paths are available?

We offer diverse career paths in platform engineering, DevOps, site reliability engineering, and cloud architecture. Our team works with cutting-edge technologies and contributes to open-source projects. We provide continuous learning opportunities, conference attendance, and certification support for professional development.

How does your platform handle enterprise-scale deployments?

Our Enterprise tier is designed for large-scale operations with unlimited Kubernetes clusters, multi-cloud management, advanced security compliance (SOC2, ISO27001), enterprise GitOps automation, and dedicated technical account management. We support Fortune 500 companies with complex infrastructure requirements.

What's your approach to platform engineering and DevOps practices?

We follow industry best practices including Infrastructure as Code, GitOps workflows, immutable infrastructure, observability-driven development, and shift-left security. Our platform enables teams to implement platform engineering principles with self-service capabilities, standardized tooling, and automated compliance.
